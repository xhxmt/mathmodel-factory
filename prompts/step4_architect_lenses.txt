---LENS_1---
OPTIMIZE FOR: EMPIRICAL RIGOR. Build the paper around the strongest causal identification strategy available in these results. The best paper is the one where a skeptic would have the hardest time challenging the empirical findings. Prefer designs with clean identification (natural experiments, discontinuities, instrumental variables) over descriptive associations. If one of the extension agents found a stronger identification approach than the main analysis, consider making that the centerpiece. Sacrifice breadth and novelty if needed to maximize the credibility of the core estimate.
---LENS_2---
OPTIMIZE FOR: NOVELTY. Build the paper around the most surprising or counterintuitive finding — the result that would make a seminar audience rethink their assumptions. The best paper is the one that overturns a widely held belief or reveals a pattern nobody expected. Look for findings that contradict conventional wisdom, reverse the expected sign, or show that a taken-for-granted relationship doesn't hold. A surprising descriptive fact with clean measurement can be more valuable than a well-identified but predictable causal estimate.
---LENS_3---
OPTIMIZE FOR: BREADTH OF CLAIM. Build the paper that makes the broadest, most generalizable argument the evidence can support. The best paper is one whose core claim applies far beyond the specific dataset or setting — one that changes how scholars think about a class of phenomena, not just this case. Weave the most findings into a single, ambitious story where each result reinforces the others. Look for the argument that connects descriptive patterns, main results, heterogeneity, and mechanisms into a unified framework with wide applicability.
---LENS_4---
Build the paper that weaves the most findings into a single, compelling story where each result reinforces the others. The best paper is one with maximum narrative coherence — where descriptive patterns, main results, heterogeneity, mechanisms, and robustness all tell a consistent, mutually reinforcing story. Look for the argument that makes all the empirical pieces fit together.
---LENS_5---
Build the paper around the findings with the largest real-world stakes. The best paper is one where the results have clear implications for how institutions, policies, or practices should change. Look for the argument where the empirical evidence speaks most directly to an active policy debate or practical decision.
