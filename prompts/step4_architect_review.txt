Focus only on the proposal's core empirical package. Your job is to say whether the proposal's main claim set is supportable as written.

Pay particular attention to:
- Whether the proposal's headline claims are supported by the strongest available evidence
- Whether any central finding depends on a narrow sample, thin treatment support, or clear unobserved confounder
- Whether any central finding is partly mechanical because treatment and outcome share a denominator, the outcome is a derived complement/residual, or the subgroup gradient mainly reflects baseline levels or room-to-move
- Whether the proposal is upgrading suggestive or descriptive results into causal or mechanism claims
- Whether any results listed as central should really be downgraded to appendix or discarded
- Whether the proposal is trying to carry too many central results instead of building a focused paper

If a proposal treats a shared-denominator share result or a derived-residual regression as an independent confirmation test, that is usually a `DOWNGRADE` or `DISCARD` unless there is separate count/level/external evidence carrying the claim.

Write your review to `paper_map_review___N__.md` with these sections:

## Overall Verdict
State exactly one verdict:
- `VERDICT: SUPPORT`
- `VERDICT: SUPPORT_WITH_DOWNGRADES`
- `VERDICT: UNSUPPORTED`

## Core Claims Audit
List each core empirical claim the proposal wants to feature in the body. For each, state:

- your assessment: `SUPPORTED`, `DOWNGRADE`, `APPENDIX_ONLY`, or `DISCARD`

## Scope And Sample Risks
Identify any sample-path opacity, treatment-support thinness, subgroup misalignment, or external-validity limit that the decider must keep in view.

## Identification And Mechanism Risks
Identify places where the proposal overstates causal identification or mechanism evidence.

## Tempting Overclaims To Forbid
List any claims or result framings that later writing steps should avoid unless new evidence appears.

## Recommendation To Decider
In one paragraph, say whether this proposal should be selected as-is, selected only if narrowed, or rejected in favor of a better-supported proposal.
